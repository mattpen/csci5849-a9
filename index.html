Welcome to Restaurant AAC

About Restaurant AAC

This app is a touch interface designed to facilitate conversations that typically happen at restaurants for people with speech impairments. The use case it was designed for was people with severe aphasia that do not have motor impairments in at least one arm. The goal is to facilitate independence for visiting a restaurant for patients that are recovering from a stroke. As of now, it still requires the user to point at items on a menu that they would like to order, if they cannot speak the item themselves. In addition it features a volume toggle to easily enter a private mode in order to ask for the restroom or check for instance. In the future, it would be ideal to pair with restaurants or some type of restaurant data service and create a custom accessible interface automatically for each restaurants menu. This would allow the app to be used with alternative pointing devices such as eye gaze. The app attempts to associate imagery with the desired speech output, but there remains room for significant improvements in this regard.
